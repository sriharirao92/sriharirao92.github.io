Dataset:¶

The dataset contains data about census demographics and economic data by tracts and counties such as county, total population, race, sex, income, poverty level and type of employment etc.

The data here are taken from the DP03 and DP05 tables of the 2015 American Community Survey 5-year estimates. The full datasets and much more can be found at the American Factfinder website. Currently.

The final dataset is a combination of multiple datasets, the datasets are:

  • "acs2015_county_data.csv": Data for each county or county equivalent in the US, including DC and Puerto Rico.

    Counties are political subdivisions, and the boundaries of some have been set for centuries. Census tracts, however, are defined by the census bureau and will have a much more consistent size. A typical census tract has around 5000 or so residents.

The current dataset is obtained from Kaggle:

    https://www.kaggle.com/muonneutrino/us-census-demographic-data

    Dataset Type: Multivariate, Table and Spatial dataset.

    The dataset is obtained from MPI (Migration policy Institute) which hosts public datasets of US migration and immigration data.
U.S. Census data on U.S. immigrants and the native born based on:
  • Demographics (size of population, age, country of birth, naturalization trends, children in immigrant families)
  • Language and education (rates of limited English proficiency, levels of educational attainment, languages spoken at home by English proficiency)
  • Workforce (immigrants' share among all workers, top occupations and industries, skill underutilization of college-educated immigrants)
  • Income (median incomes, income distributions, poverty rates)

Population and Income Analysis¶

4) Question 4: Which states and counties contribute more towards population and economic system of the country?¶

Data Abstraction:

  • Attributes - State, County, TotalPop, IncomePerCap
  • Derived Attributes - Here I have derived a new attribute named "Region". The data from acs2015_county_data.csv is joined with US region data obtained from US Census board. The join is done based on the "Key - States" United States of America is divided into 4 major regions named "Northeast", "Midwest", "South" and "West".

Actions: See which regions, states and counties contribute more towards aggregated population and income per capita observations.

Targets: Viewing population contribution using the hierarchical structure of the US regions, states, county and quantitative attributes for population and income data.

Visual encoding: Here I have adopted a version of the Treemap which incorporates Zooming capabilities (i.e Navigation or changing viewpoint). Utilizing the hierarchical structure of the state and county, we can create these kinds of tree maps to clearly visualize the population of each region of each state and county to economic system.

In order to generate this visual, We created a JSON file from the csv file as it is easier to enumerate the data and use in D3.js. For this visualization I opted for D3.js as it proivides more control over the visualization and to create custom visualization such as a zoomable Treemap.

  • How Zoomable Treemap works - The treemap by default shows population and incomePerCapita data of each US region, but it allows for users to drill down deeper into each state and county level data. As user clicks on a Region the Treemap drills into state level and displays the population of each state in the region, on drilling down further we can see the population of counties in that state.

  • Advantages - The Zooming capability adopted in a Treemap allows reduce clutter (making use of the hierarchy) and makes it more easier to view. A classic treemap would have lot of clutter.

Deductions: From the map we can how the total population is spread out throughout the country and also easily visualize that

  • South region has the highest population count with 127.7 million people which occupies 40.35% of the total population. Drilling down further we can see that Delaware has the least population in the south region of US.
  • Northeast has the least amount of population with New York housing the highest population of the region with 19.7 million people (which is about 6.21% of total population).
  • The west and Mid-west regions fall between the above two regions with second and third highest population in US.
